# Signal Readout

Signal Readout is an early diagnostic process for making human-AI patterns visible inside organizational work.

It is designed to help teams notice what is changing before those changes become invisible culture.

## Purpose

The purpose of a Signal Readout is to translate dispersed organizational responses into a structured view of emerging human-AI phenomena.

It helps identify:

- early signals
- concern patterns
- belief patterns
- phenomenon candidates
- trajectory hints
- stewardship needs
- recommended next moves

## What It Is Not

Signal Readout is not:

- a technical audit
- a compliance checklist
- a maturity score alone
- a generic AI adoption survey
- a replacement for human judgment

It is a structured observation and interpretation process.

## What It Looks For

A Signal Readout may look for signals such as:

- increasing reliance on AI outputs without review
- reduced reconstruction of reasoning
- improved output without proportional learning
- loss of clarity about responsibility
- uncertainty about what should remain human
- overconfidence in fluent answers
- hidden pressure to automate
- concern about judgment, dignity, purpose, or agency
- positive examples of inquiry, reflection, and collective learning

## Readout Flow

```text
Responses -> Signals -> Phenomenon Mapping -> Steward Interpretation -> Recommended Assets
```

The readout does not only diagnose risk.

It also identifies where an organization can nourish positive capacities:

- judgment
- inquiry
- deliberation
- learning
- stewardship
- institutional memory
- human-AI mutualism

## Possible Outputs

A Signal Readout may produce:

- a signal snapshot
- a concern and belief map
- a phenomenon readout
- a trajectory note
- a steward interpretation brief
- recommended mitigation and nourishment assets
- suggested next moves

## Why It Matters

AI adoption often becomes visible through outputs first.

Livingtelligence asks what is happening underneath:

- What is changing in judgment?
- What is changing in learning?
- What is changing in trust?
- What is changing in responsibility?
- What is changing in the organization's ability to deliberate and correct?

Signal Readout is a first step toward making those changes observable and governable.
