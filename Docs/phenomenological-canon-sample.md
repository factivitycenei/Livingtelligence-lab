# Phenomenological Canon Sample

Livingtelligence works with a growing canon of human-AI phenomena observed or inferred from real work, organizational practice, and the evolving relationship between human and artificial intelligence.

The canon is not a closed inventory. It is a living vocabulary for making emerging patterns visible, discussable, and actionable.

## Why A Canon

Organizations often experience AI-related changes before they have language for them.

Without language, patterns can become normalized as culture.

The phenomenological canon gives teams a way to:

- recognize what is changing
- discuss it without reducing it to generic "AI adoption"
- connect patterns to signals and consequences
- select mitigation and nourishment assets
- preserve judgment, learning, agency, and institutional coherence

## Sample Phenomena

### Cognitive Surrender

When assistance begins to replace judgment.

This appears when people increasingly accept AI-supported outputs without reconstructing reasons, testing assumptions, or exercising independent criterion.

Potential response:

- judgment-first protocols
- reconstruction practices
- steward review moments
- deliberation-first workflows

### Achievement Gap

When output improves but capacity does not.

This appears when people produce better results with AI but do not gain proportional understanding, skill, ownership, or reusable capability.

Potential response:

- learning reconstruction
- process reflection
- capability checks
- authorship and ownership practices

### Purpose Displacement

When work continues to function but no longer orients in the same way.

This appears when tasks still get done, but the meaning, direction, or human purpose of the work becomes less visible.

Potential response:

- purpose review
- value interrogation
- role redesign
- stewardship conversations

### Algorithmic Sycophancy

When validation weakens correction.

This appears when AI confirms what people want to hear, over-validates assumptions, or reduces the friction required for truth-seeking.

Potential response:

- adversarial review
- verification workflows
- disagreement prompts
- reality checkpoints

### Epistemic Warrant Collapse

When fluency detaches from reality.

This appears when confident, fluent, or persuasive AI-generated language is treated as if it carried evidence, grounding, or truth.

Potential response:

- source checks
- non-textual evidence requirements
- reality checkpoint protocol
- confidence and warrant mapping

### Value Interrogation

When AI adoption forces an organization to ask what values it wants automation to amplify.

This appears when teams realize that automation does not scale neutral processes. It scales existing values, incentives, assumptions, and blind spots.

Core question:

> What values are we scaling?

Potential response:

- value interrogation workshop
- encoded values diagnostic
- dignity-centered governance principles
- redesign before automation

### Human-AI Mutualism

When AI strengthens inquiry, reflection, and collective learning rather than replacing judgment.

This is a positive phenomenon. It appears when AI helps people ask better questions, compare perspectives, test reasoning, and deepen collective intelligence.

Potential nourishment:

- inquiry-first workflows
- human-AI reflection practices
- collective learning loops
- steward-supported sensemaking

## From Phenomena To Action

Livingtelligence does not treat phenomena as labels only.

Each phenomenon can be connected to:

- signals
- readouts
- risks
- positive capacities
- stewards
- mitigation assets
- nourishment assets
- governance questions

The goal is to move from recognition to responsible action.
